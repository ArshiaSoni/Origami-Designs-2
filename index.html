Some of the designs made by me

Camel

Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms. Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.

Chameleon

Almost half of the species of chameleons live in Madagascar. Chameleons vary widely in the term of sizes.

Cicada

There are around 3,000 cicada species. Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes. Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.

Panda

Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Pigeon

Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time. Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.

Teddy

The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is a 55-foot-4 creation was made in 2008. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
